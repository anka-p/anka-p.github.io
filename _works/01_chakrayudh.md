I. Composition and Visual Rhythm
The composition is built upon a complex visual rhythm created through the repetition of organic, flowing lines. These gestural marks guide the viewer’s eye across the picture plane, creating a sense of dynamic movement and flow. The arrangement of forms suggests a flowing repetition, reminiscent of natural growth or atmospheric phenomena.

II. Chromatic Strategy
Central to the work is a saturated, high-chroma palette. By juxtaposing complementary hues, the artist creates a sense of chromatic tension and optical vibrancy. The use of layered pigments adds significant spatial depth, transforming the two-dimensional surface into a recessive, three-dimensional space. The progressive rhythm of color—moving from intense, undiluted tones to subtle, mixed values—evokes a specific emotional or meditative state.

III. Technique and Materiality
The work emphasizes the materiality of the traditional medium through expressive, visible brushwork. Techniques such as impasto create a tactile surface texture that serves as a physical record of the creative process. This emphasis on the "hand of the artist" bridges the gap between representational form and abstract expression.
